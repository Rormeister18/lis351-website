Bad Bunny's Super Bowl Halftime Show

A Halftime Show That Shifted the Culture

The Performance

The performance displayed a statement about Latin identity and love over hate.

Viewership

138 million viewers tuned in to watch Bad Bunny make history.

The Language

This was the first Super Bowl Halftime Show almost entirely in Spanish

Why It Matters

The Super Bowl is regularly one of the most viewed events in the United States every year.

The Super Bowl this year reached the highest viewership of any Super Bowl yet, maxing out at around 138 million. With that many eyes on the screen artists want to make an impact with the 10 minutes they are allowed on screen and Bad Bunny did just that. The halftime show was always a fun break while both teams rest for the final stretch, but with how commercialized the game has become, it is now a highlight for many viewers.

What's Different About This Year

Bad Bunny is a Latin trap and reggaeton artist who is one of the largest artists currently on the planet. With smash hits such as Tití Me Preguntó and DtMF it is easy to see why he was an easy choice to perform.

There has been some controversy around this years performance, however. All of Bad Bunny's music is in Spanish meaning the majority of English viewers may not be able to understand his music. This has led to many people saying they won't be viewing the show and even going so far as to say he should not be performing in it.

Also, Turning Point USA announced that it would host an "All-American Halftime Show" as an alternative option with headliner Kid Rock.
